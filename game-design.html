Electric Alps


The game I worked on as an intern Game Design at Dirty Paws Studio. I helped in designing the progression & reward systems the game will feature, and blockouts and the mechanical progression of the tutorial. More info about the game

Designing these systems was a very good exercise in communication, as I joined a team in progress and had to understand their vision and what the various systems I was designing had to achieve. I went through many iterations of designing, asking and integrating feedback. In the end, I designed the overall progression of the game and the various reward and progression systems that the game will feature.

I also helped in designing the tutorial of the game. My job was to figure out what and how the players learn in the tutorial, and then create sketches for the levels. I ended up creating 12 levels.

A Beesy Week



A Beesy Week is a boardgame designed for the Sustainability Game Jam, which had as topic as the name implies sustainability. The game is a co-op boardgame where players work together as bees to polinate as many flowers as possible before the end of the week. It was specifically designed to include children in the target audience. I did the game design of the game.

The main challenge was that our target audience included kids aged 8-12 years old. Understanding the abilities and what children like was hence an important part of the design, to make sure the game was intuitive enough and that children would like it. At the same time, we did not want to forego all complexity, so that adults can also enjoy the game, and play together with their children. We solved this by mostly creating vertical complexity, which meant the base game is easy to understand and complete, but the game allows for optimization of gameplay.

This is reflected in the end condition, which specifically states "Polinate as many flowers as possible". Initially, the game had a target that players needed to beat, which introduced a win and loss condition, but after playtesting, it became evident that this did not work out. It would either be not enjoyable for adults, because children should be able to beat the game, or the opposite. An open condition allows players of any skill level to play and enjoy the game, and specifically children to keep playing and improve, while never feeling like the game punishes you.


Snek.Game



A game made for the Klagenfurt x Coventry gamejam, and later polished into a small puzzle game. The player plays as a snek, who has to push boxes around and eat berries to complete levels. The game was inspired by What The Golf, and much like in its fashion, every level becomes a bit more ridicilous than the last one. I worked together with two friends, and designed most of the levels.

We ended up designing around 20 levels 10 unique mechanics, slowly introducing them one by one as to not overwhelm the player. The later levels also include some mechanics that play off previous mechanics to suprise the player.


Super Mario Maker 2


A 2D platformer level editor in which I have made many "troll" levels. Troll levels are a special type of level trying to flip the classic mario formula on its head. They try to predict what the player will do and punishes them for it, in an entertaining manner. Combining clever traps with a healthy dose of slapstick, troll levels aim to constantly subvert the expectations of the player. This type of level is something of a happy accident resulting from the simplistic complexity of the Mario Maker games, and over the past six years, a large community has grown around them, of which I've had the pleasure to be a part of. My levels are often considered among the best in this subgenre, and have been played/seen over 14 million times.

Arguably the most crucial element of designing such levels is iteration and playtesting, to make sure a level's predictions actually match what players do and to refine the cues the level gives the player so they don't get lost. The Salt Mines for example took over 30 revisions, scrapping and rebuilding entire sections many times based on playtests.

Bagels of War


Bagels of War is a tower defense battle game, inspired by Plants VS Zombies, Battle Cats and Kingdom Rush. Players have to survive the enemy onslaught of bagels while building up an economy to finance defensive structures and build armies of their own. The game features many different units and structures that players can strategically choose to bring to battle. During battle, players can also level up their units, allowing for a different approach each level.

This is my main project at the moment, an indie game scheduled for release July 2024. Most of the programming and is ready to start making content for the game, which is what I am currently working on. I am also working together two artists and a sound designer to design the assets for the game.

As the game features over 20 different units and structures, over 100 upgrades and multiple systems that increase player power, this is a very good exercise of balancing, to ensure a smooth difficulty curve, progression path for the player and many viable strategies are possible.


Gregs Dream


Gregs dream is a metroidvania that I have worked on with an artist/sound designer friend for over a year. It is about a young boy named Greg who gets imprisoned in a nightmare and has to find a way to escape. It is inspired by classic zelda dungeons and other metroidvanias like Hollow Knight. I do the game and level design, and the programming.

The game currently features over two hours of gameplay, but still has a long way to go. It was our first attempt at a bigger, marketable game, but fell prey to scope creep and overambition, hence it currenly lays dormant in my pile of unfinished projects


Dungeon of the Mad Dungeon Master


A boardgame designed by me and a few friends for university. In Dungeon of the Mad Dungeon Master, a party of adventurers is desperately trying to escape an ever changing dungeon. One player takes up the role as the dungeon master, while the rest play as adventurers. However, unlike Dungeons and Dragons, in this game the dungeon master's goal is to actually kill the players by creating the deadliest dungeon possible. Adventurers will face monsters, activate traps, obtain treasure and traverse different biomes. The fun doesn't stop when the players die, they turn into ghosts that can still help their allies from the other side.

I together with a friend did the game design. We really liked the game as a team, but we heavily underestimated the logistics and work required to publish a boardgame, so the game never made it past two physical versions and some playtests. There is ambition to one day pick it up again. DotDM features 4 playable characters with over 50 unique action cards, 8 monsters, 8 treasures and 16 dungeon rooms.
