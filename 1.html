Electric Alps


The game I worked on as an intern Game Design at Dirty Paws Studio. I helped in designing the progression & reward systems the game will feature, and blockouts and the mechanical progression of the tutorial. More info about the game

Designing these systems was a very good exercise in communication, as I joined a team in progress and had to understand their vision and what the various systems I was designing had to achieve. I went through many iterations of designing, asking and integrating feedback. In the end, I designed the overall progression of the game and the various reward and progression systems that the game will feature.

I also helped in designing the tutorial of the game. My job was to figure out what and how the players learn in the tutorial, and then create sketches for the levels. I ended up creating 12 levels.

A level I designed. This level aims to teach the player about branching conduits, and is set up in a way that helps the player figure this mechanic out without being too explicit.

The Salt Mines


What I consider my best level. It was my attempt at pushing the genre and engine itself to its limits. It uses some mechanism to change up the level between player deaths, which is usually thought off to be impossible. This gives a whole new set of expectations to subvert. The level took over 150 hours to make, and another 50 of playtesting and revising. It takes around one hour to complete.

Gamejam Games


This is a small showcase of some of my personal favorite gamejam projects that I participated in. You can find my other gamejam games on my itch.io.

Snek.Game


A puzzle game made for the Klagenfurt x Coventry gamejam. The player plays as a snek, who has to push boxes around and eat berries to complete levels. The game was inspired by What The Golf, and much like in its fashion, every level becomes a bit more ridicilous than the last one.

The jam lasted 80 hours, and we ended up designing around 20 levels with over 10 unique mechanics. I did the majority of the game design and level design. We are very happy with how the project came out.



A Beesy Week


A Beesy Week is a boardgame designed for the Sustainability Game Jam, which had as topic as the name implies sustainability. The game is a co-op boardgame where players work together as bees to polinate as many flowers as possible before the end of the week. It was specifically designed to include children in the target audience.

The jam lasted 1.5 days, and by the end of it, we had a one physical prototype of the game. I did the game design. The main mechanic which I like is the changing board between turns, which adds a lot of replay value to an otherwise small game.



Bertle Royale


A game made for the Klagenfurt Winter 6th Winter Game Jam. The game is themed after Gamebert, which is the mascotte of the jam. The game is a card game designed for 3-5 players where players play different Gameberts trying to survive until the last Bert stands. Players have coins, and are eliminated if they run out of coins. The tricky part is however that you have to play a Bert during your turn, but Berts have a coin cost, so choose wisely which Berts to play.

The jam lasted 72 hours, managed to get second place in the Game Design, Fun and Overall categories, and third place in Creativity. I did the game design with a friend.



Super Mario Maker 2


A 2D platformer level editor in which I have made many "troll" levels. Troll levels are a special type of level trying to flip the classic mario formula on its head. They try to predict what the player will do and punishes them for it, in an entertaining manner. Combining clever traps with a healthy dose of slapstick, troll levels aim to constantly subvert the expectations of the player. Player communication and the playtesting and revision process are crucial elements, both to make sure a level's predictions actually match what players do and to refine the cues the level gives the player so they don't get lost. This type of level is something of a happy accident resulting from the simplistic complexity of the Mario Maker games, and over the past six years, a large community has grown around them, of which I've had the pleasure to be a part of.

Bagels of War


Bagels of War is a tower defense battle game, inspired by Plants VS Zombies, Battle Cats and Kingdom Rush. Players have to survive the enemy onslaught of bagels while building up an economy to finance defensive structures and build armies of their own. The game features many different units and structures that players can strategically choose to bring to battle. During battle, players can also level up their units, allowing for a different approach each level.

This is my main project at the moment, an indie game scheduled for release July 2024. Most of the programming and is ready to start making content for the game, which is what I am currently working on. I am also working together two artists and a sound designer to design the assets for the game.
